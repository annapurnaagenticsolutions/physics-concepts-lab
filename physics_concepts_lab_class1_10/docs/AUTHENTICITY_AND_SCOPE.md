# Physics Concepts Lab — Authenticity, Scope, and Trust Statement

**Release:** v0.2-authenticated-prototype  
**Status:** QA-authenticated prototype / selected concept release  
**Date:** 2026-06-12

## What this lab honestly claims

- It presents selected Class 1–10 concepts in a visual, learner-friendly way.
- It has been checked for obvious factual errors, unsafe claims, and over-simplified language that could mislead learners.
- It follows an India-facing school-learning baseline using NCERT/CBSE as the reference direction.
- It is designed to support curiosity, explanation, practice, and confidence.

## What this lab does not claim

- It is not an official NCERT/CBSE product.
- It is not a complete syllabus, textbook, exam-prep guarantee, or certification course.
- It does not replace teachers, parents, textbooks, laboratory supervision, or formal school assessment.
- It should not be used for medical, financial, engineering, electrical, chemical, or safety decisions.

## Source discipline

Primary reference direction:

1. NCERT official textbook portal: https://www.ncert.nic.in/textbook.php
2. CBSE Academic curriculum/syllabus portal: https://cbseacademic.nic.in/

## Corrections and additions made in this QA-authenticated pass

- Expanded Physics from 32 to 40 lessons in the prior QA pass.
- Added caveats for magnets, heat transfer, electricity-bill estimates, and safe low-voltage circuits.
- Kept prototype label because Physics still needs a second visual-interaction QA pass.

## Safety and age-fit rules

- Keep activities observational unless explicitly safe.
- Use adult/teacher supervision for electricity, heat, glass, tools, outdoor observation, food handling, chemicals, and health/body topics.
- Treat models and diagrams as learning models, not exact pictures of reality.
- Label estimates as estimates when real-world values depend on location, tariff, weather, materials, or conditions.
